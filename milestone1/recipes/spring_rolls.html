Vietnamese Spring Rolls

Credit: Vietnamese Spring Roll Recipe by Lauren Allen

Prep Time: 20 min | Cook Time: 5 min | Assembly Time: 15 min

Total Time: 40 min

Servings: 15 servings

Ingredients

Main

  • 1 package of rice paper
  • 1 package of vermicelli rice noodles
  • 2 mangos (peeled and cut into thin slices)
  • 1 large carrot (peeled and cut into thin strips)
  • 1 large cucumber (peeled and thinly sliced)
  • 1 pound of small, cooked shrimp (deveined and tails removed)
  • 1 bunch of mint leaves
  • 1 bunch of basil leaves
  • 1 bunch of cilantro

Peanut Sauce

  • 3/4 cup of sweet chili sauce
  • 1/3 cup of peanut butter
  • 1/2 tsp of soy sauce
  • 1/2 tsp of hoisin sauce

Instructions

  1. Cook vermicelli noodles in boiling water according to package instructions. Drain and rinse with cold water.
  2. Gather all topping ingredients together (including chopped veggies, herbs, cooked shrimp).
  3. Add about 1 inch of water to a large, deep dish. Place one rice paper into the water and soak for 10 to 15 seconds (it should still feel firm as it is removed and laid on the plate.
  4. Layer 1 to 2 slices of each veggie, a few shrimp, a few leaves of each herb, and a pinch of noodles on 1/3 of the rice paper closest to you. The rice paper will soften as the topping ingredients are added. If soaked too long, the rice paper will tear when rolled up.
  5. Fold the sides of the spring roll in over the ingredients. Pull the side closest to you up and over the ingredients, sealing everything together tightly and rolling it up.
  6. Add all of the peanut sauce ingredients into a food processor or blender and pulse until smooth.
