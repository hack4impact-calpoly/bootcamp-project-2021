Vermicelli Noodle Bowl


Details

Credit: Vermicelli Noodle Bowl Recipe by Caitlin Shoemaker

Prep Time: 20 min | Cook Time: 5 min | Total Time: 25 min | Servings: 3 to 4 servings

Ingredients

  • 1 batch of tofu
  • 4 oz of vermicelli noodles
  • 1/4 leafy green lettuce head (chopped)
  • 1 medium carrot (finely shredded or grated)
  • 2 cucumbers (thinly sliced)
  • 1/2 red bell pepper (thinly sliced)
  • 1 cup of bean sprouts
  • 1/2 cup of chopped fresh herbs (cilantro, mint, basil, and/or perilla)
  • 1/4 cup of dry roasted peanuts

  • 1/4 cup of unseasoned rice vinegar
  • Juice of 1/2 lime
  • 2 tbsp of soy sauce
  • 1/4 cup of sweet chili sauce
  • 1 clove of garlic (finely minced or grated)
  • 1-2 tbsp of water (to thin if necessary)

Add an Ingredient



Instructions

  1. Make the dressing: add the vinegar, lime juice, soy sauce, sweet chili sauce, and garlic to a jar with a tight-fitting lid and shake well to combine. If the dressing is thicker than preferred, add 1 to 2 tbsp of water to thin. Set aside.
  2. Soak the noodles: bring 3 cups of water to a boil. Place the vermicelli noodles in a wide-bottom bowl, then carefully pour the water over them. Let soak for 3 to 5 minutes, or until the noodles are tender. Drain and set aside.
  3. Assemble: place the noodles and lettuce in the bottom of each serving bowl, then top with tofu, carrot, cucumber, bell pepper, bean sprouts, fresh herbs, and peanuts.
  4. Serve: drizzle each bowl with dressing, then serve as desired. Leftovers will keep in the fridge for up to 3 days.
