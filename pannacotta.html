back home, panna cotta was my mother's favorite dessert. in the mornings, before my classes would start, i would bike to the groceries, pick up cream, and let it set in the fridge while i was in class, so when she was finished with work, it would be ready for her.

this yummy italian dessert is a sweet cream thickened with gelatin, and traditionally flavored with vanilla and served with honey. however, this take incorporates the delicate aroma of almond, to lend another layer of mellow complexity to the dessert. it brings warmth to the cold dish, and evokes some of the aromatic qualities associated with india and the culinary culture.

i n g r e d i e n t s

serves 6

  • 1.5 cups whole milk
  • 3 tsps powdered gelatin
  • 0.5 cups honey (this can be adjusted to your taste)
  • 1.5 cups heavy cream
  • 1 tsp almond extract
  • a few drops of vanilla extract
  • a pinch of salt
  • garnishes: honey, almonds


i n s t r u c t i o n s

  1. pour the cold milk into a saucepan, sprinkle the gelatin over it, and allow it to sit for 5 minutes
  2. after 5 minutes, turn the heat on low and stir for 1-2 minutes, until the gelatin has dissolved
  3. add the honey and stir until incorporation for about 30 seconds (note: don't let the milk get too warm)
  4. take the saucepan off the heat and mix in the rest of the ingredients
  5. pour the mixture into 6 ramekins (i use a muffin tin bc i dont have these lol). make sure to grease them with spray or butter for easy removal!
  6. top with honey, almonds, toffee, or whatever else makes you happy
  7. share with someone you love
