Simple Loco Moco

Loco moco is a dish featured in contemporary Hawaiian cuisine. There are many variations, but the traditional loco moco consists of white rice, topped with a hamburger, a fried egg, and brown gravy. Variations may include bacon, ham, Spam, tofu, kalua pork, Portuguese sausage, teriyaki beef, teriyaki chicken, mahi-mahi, shrimp, oysters, and other meats. The recipe below is designed to act as a guide, and not something to be followed prescriptively. Play with your food! Adjust amounts based on your tastes.

Serves 2 people

Various

  • 2 (4 ounce) patties ground beef
  • 1 pinch of salt and freshly ground black pepper to taste
  • 1 pinch of cayenne pepper
  • 2 eggs
  • 2 cups hot cooked white rice

  • 1 ½ cups beef stock
  • 4 teaspoons soy sauce
  • 1 teaspoon Worcestershire sauce
  • 2 drops sesame oil, or to taste
  • 2 teaspoons unsalted butter, divided
  • 4 teaspoons cornstarch to thicken
  • 2 tablespoons minced green onions, white and light green parts only

    1. Season patties with salt, pepper, and cayenne.
    2. Whisk beef stock, soy sauce, Worcestershire sauce, sesame oil, ketchup, sugar, and cornstarch together in a bowl.
    3. Melt butter in a skillet over medium-high heat. Cook patty in the hot skillet until a browned crust forms on the bottom, about 5 minutes. Flip; toss in green onions and cook the other side until both beef and onions are browned, 4 to 5 minutes more. Remove patty and most of the green onions to a plate.
    4. Pour beef stock mixture into the skillet. Stir, scraping up browned bits from the bottom, until thickened, about 2 minutes. Reduce heat to low and reserve gravy until needed.
    5. Melt remaining butter in another skillet over medium heat. Add eggs to the skillet and fry side-by-side until whites are set and yolks are still runny.
    6. Divide rice between 2 serving bowls. Place patties over the rice, spoon gravy on top, and add the fried eggs!
