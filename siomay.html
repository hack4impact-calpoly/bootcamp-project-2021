Siomay

Description

Siomay is another classic Indonesian dish. They are similar to dumplings. What makes them really good is the peanut sauce. This was the first dish I learned how to make. The recipe below can feed a party of people! Peanut allergies beware!

Ingredients

Note: There are no measurements on purpose. All ingredients are to the cook's discretion and a lot of taste testing!

  • Fish Paste 1lb
  • Ground pork 2lb
  • Green Onions
  • Water chestnut 1 can
  • Tapioca Starch 1 pack
  • Medium Shumai wrap
  • Extra Firm Tofu
  • Eggs
  • Potatoes
  • Sesame Oil
  • Knorr Chicken Bouillon
  • Peanut Sauce package from asian markets

Instructions

  1. Cut potatoes and tofu into quarts.
  2. Steam potatoes and tofu for 45 minutes.
  3. Boil hard boiled eggs.
  4. Get a mixing bowl and add the fish paste, ground pork, chopped green onions, and water chestnuts.
  5. Mix ingredients well. Add one egg. Mix again.
  6. Add one tablespoon of sesame oil.
  7. Add five tablespoon of chicken bouillon.
  8. Add increments of tapioca starch while mixing until completely mixed in.
  9. Put one spoonful of meat into each wrap. Refer to the picture to see how to make it.
  10. Once all folded, steam for 45 minutes.
  11. Make the peanut sauce from the asian market
  12. Done!
