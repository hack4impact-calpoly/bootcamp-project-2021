Blueberry Pie


Everybody's favorite pie done right. Yields 8 servings if you can stand the idea of sharing!

This household favorite blueberry pie acts as the perfect dessert. Serve topped with vanilla ice cream or eat on its own.

The perfectly delicious, not too sweet filling stuffed into a beautifully baked crispy, laticed pie satisfies your eyes before it ever even reaches your mouth.


Ingredients

  • pie dough for double 9 inch pie crust, chilled
  • 5 cups fresh blueberries
  • 1 tbsp freshly squeezed lemon juice
  • 2 tbsp milk or cream
  • 4 tbsp all purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup brown sugar
  • 1/4 tsp ground cinnamon
  • 1 large egg, beaten with 1 tbsp water to make egg wash

Instructions

  1. Butter a 9 inch pie pan. On a lighly floured surface, roll out half of dough to 1/8 inch thick circle. Fit into buttered pie pan and trim
  2. For lattice top, roll remaining half of dough 1/8 inch thick into rectangles approximately 11x14 inches. Cover with plastic wrap and let chill
  3. Preheat oven to 400 degrees Farenheit. Place baking sheet on bottom rack
  4. In a large bowl, whisk together lemon juice, milk, and flour, whisking until smooth. Stir in brown sugar and cinnamon and add blueberries. Let sit for 5 minutes
  5. Brush the rim of the crust with the egg wash, place the top crust on, trim to 1/2 inch larger than size of pan and weave strips of dough to create lattice top. Crimp edges to seal
  6. Brush crust with egg wash, sprinkle lightly with sugar and place pie on baking sheet and bake for 20 minutes
  7. Reduce heat to 375 degrees Farenheit and transfer baking sheet to center rack. Cover rim of pie crust with foil. Bake for 35 minutes.
  8. Let cool to set and thicken before serving
