Cacio e Pepe

Spaghetti Cacio e Pepe is a classic Italian pasta dish and one of my personal favorites. It's easy to understand why when you mix two of the best things in the world, cheese and pepper. The secret to this recipe is to use pecorino cheese, which won't lump up when making the sauce, and to use crushed whole peppercorns, for a strong peppery aroma! On top of this, we will use a slow whisking method when adding the cream so that we absorb all the flavor of the powerful pecorino cheese.

Ingredients

  • 0.7 lb spaghetti
  • Black pepper corns, to taste (make sure you have a good amount)
  • 7 oz Pecorino Romano cheese (to be grated)
  • Salt, to taste

Preparation

  1. Grate the pecorino cheese and move to the side for later.
  2. Boil water in a pan, half as much water as you would usually use to cook pasta because we want the starch for later. Once it reaches a boil you can add salt to taste. Cook the spaghetti very al dente and keep the water afterwards.
  3. In the meantime, place the peppercorns on a cutting board and crush them with a pestle or a grinder (or any other tool you want).
  4. Pour half of the crushed pepper into a large non-stick pan.
  5. Toast over low heat stirring with a wood spoon then add a couple ladles of pasta water (from the spaghetti). The bubbles you see are from the starch in the water.
  6. When the pasta is done (very al dente), pour it into the pan with the toasted pepper and let it cook while stirring continuosly. Add another ladle or two of water if necessary.
  7. As you stir, keep pouring ladles of water only when the pan is almost completely dry. When the pasta is cooked you can move on to making the Pecorino cream.
  8. Pour half of the grated Pecorino cheese into a bowl and add a ladle of pasta water. Stir vigorously with a whisk. Add more water when needed.
  9. The add the rest of the Pecorino cheese, keeping some aside to garnish with later.
  10. Add cheese/water as needed to create a cream without lumps, it may take some careful adjustments to get the right consistency.
  11. Finish cooking the pasta, adding a little more hot water if necessary.
  12. Briefly stir your Pecorino cream over the steam of the pan with hot water, so the cream approaches a temperature similar to the pasta.
  13. Turn the heat off in the pan and pour in the Pecorino cream, stirring continuously with the tongs. Also add the remaining grated pecorino that you put aside from earlier.
  14. Stir and saut√© the pasta again, then move it to a plate.
  15. Enjoy your creamy and peppery Cacio e Pepe!
