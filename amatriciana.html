Spaghetti all'Amatriciana

Spaghetti all'Amatriciana is a classic Italian dsh from the Lazio region, and with all its history comes a lot of disagreement about what makes up a true Amatriciana sauce. People argue whether it is made with onions, garlic, or neither, whether you use bacon or guanciale, and whether you eat it with spaghetti or bucatini.

In this recipe, we will desribe how to make Spaghetti all'Amatriciana with the classic guanciale (cured pork cheek) along with a fresh chili pepper to add a depth of flavor!

Ingredients

  • 0.7 lb Spaghetti
  • 0.9 lb Peeled tomatoes
  • 5 oz Guanciale
  • 3/4 cup Pecorino Romano cheese (for grating)
  • Salt, to taste
  • Extra virgin olive oil, to taste
  • 1 Fresh chili pepper
  • 1/4 cup White wine

Preparation

  1. Boil water to cook the spaghetti in, then add salt + pasta when it comes to a boil. Cook the spaghetti al dente.
  2. Take the guanciale and remove the pork rind (which can be saved for use in other recipes).
  3. Cut the guaciale into 1cm thick slices and then into small strips about 1/2 cm wide
  4. Heat a drizzle of oil in a pan and add the whole chili pepper.
  5. Add the strips of guanciale and brown over low heat for 7-8 minutes, until the fat has melted and the meat is crunchy, stir often to prevent burning.
  6. Once the fat is melted, pour in the white wine, turn up the heat and let the liquid evaporate. Transfer the guanciale to a plate.
  7. Pour the peeled tomatoes into the same pan, breaking them up with your hands as you do so.
  8. Continue cooking the sauce, now with the tomatoes, for about 10 minutes.
  9. Add salt to taste and remove the whole chili pepper from the sauce
  10. Add the guanciale strips to the pan and stir to mix.
  11. Once the spaghetti is cooked, drain it and add it directly to the sauce in the pan.
  12. Saut√© the pasta very quickly to mix it with the sauce, If you like your pasta al dente you can turn off the heat, otherwise pour a little bit of the pasta water in to continue cooking.
  13. To finish, top your pasta with grated Pecorino Romano cheese.
  14. Enjoy your tasty Spaghetti all'Amatriciana!
